Best Times to Study for the SAT (Ranked)

Summers are ideal because students aren’t juggling heavy coursework and extracurriculars.

  1. Summer before Junior Year (Ideal)
    SAT Math covers content through Algebra II; waiting until Senior year risks forgetting fundamentals. Reading speed, comprehension, and vocabulary change little from Junior to Senior year, so earlier prep is just as effective. Studying now provides more testing opportunities and less pressure; the next summer can focus on applications.
  2. Summer before Senior Year
    By this point most math/grammar concepts have been covered in school, with multiple test dates still available before applications. A strong choice if earlier prep wasn’t feasible.
  3. Senior Fall
    Senior-year grades often weigh less in admissions (especially for early decision). Fall prep still allows testing before key deadlines—less ideal than summer, but better than mid–Junior year.
  4. Junior Year (Least Ideal)
    Junior year is typically the heaviest academically. SAT prep often detracts from core classes and activities unless the schedule is unusually light.
