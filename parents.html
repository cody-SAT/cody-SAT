Best Times to Study for the SAT (Ranked)

Summers are ideal because students aren’t juggling heavy coursework and extracurriculars.

  1. Summer before Junior Year (Ideal)
    Math on the SAT covers content through Algebra II; waiting until Senior year risks forgetting earlier material. Reading speed, comprehension, and vocabulary change little from Junior to Senior year, so earlier prep is just as effective. Studying now gives more testing opportunities and lowers pressure, while the next summer can be used for college applications.
  2. Summer before Senior Year
    By this point most math/grammar concepts have been covered in school, and there are still multiple test dates available before applications. Great option if earlier prep wasn’t feasible.
  3. Senior Fall
    Senior-year grades often weigh less in admissions (especially for early decision), so using fall for SAT prep can work well. Not as flexible as summer, but preferable to mid–Junior year for most students.
  4. Junior Year (Least Ideal)
    Junior year is typically the heaviest academically. SAT prep can cannibalize time from core classes and activities. Unless the schedule is unusually light, it’s the least efficient time to study.
