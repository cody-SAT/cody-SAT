Best Times to Study for the SAT (Ranked)

Summers are the ideal time to take the SAT because the student does not have to worry about coursework and extracurriculars.

  1. Summer prior to Junior year - The Math section covers up to Algebra II. If the student waits until Senior year, they may forget and have to relearn old concepts. Reading speed, comprehension, vocabulary, etc. will typically only marginally improve from Junior to Senior year, so studying earlier is worthwhile, as it gives the student more chances to take the test and less pressure as a result. Additionally, this timeline leaves the following Summer fully dedicated to college application preparations.
  2. Summer prior to Senior year - The student has learned the most concepts in school by this point and still has 5 chances to take the test.
  3. Senior Fall - Senior year grades are weighed much less in admissions than other years and may not be reviewed for early decision applicants, so Senior Fall is generally better than Junior year.
  4. Junior year (worst) - The worst time to study for the SAT is during Junior year. Any time studying for the SAT would be better spent focusing on school, unless the student has a really easy schedule.
